COLLEGE ADMISSION PROCESS - GENERAL GUIDE (Nepal Context, LICT/Bachelor Level Example)

1. Eligibility Criteria
Most Bachelor level IT/Computer Science programs (BSc.CSIT, BIT, BCA, BE Computer) require:
- Completion of +2 (Grade 12) or equivalent with Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics (for BSc.CSIT/BE Computer) or Management/Science stream (for BIT/BCA).
- Minimum GPA requirement, usually around 2.0-2.4 GPA in +2, varies by university and college.
- Some programs require an entrance examination conducted by the affiliating university (e.g., Tribhuvan University CSIT entrance, Pokhara University entrance).

2. Application Steps
Step 1: Collect the college's admission notice from its official website or notice board.
Step 2: Fill out the online/offline application form with academic transcripts, citizenship/birth certificate, and passport-size photos.
Step 3: Pay the application fee.
Step 4: Appear for the entrance examination (if applicable) which usually tests Mathematics, English, General Science, and IQ/logical reasoning.
Step 5: Check the merit list / entrance result published by the college or university.
Step 6: Complete the enrollment process by paying the admission fee and submitting original documents for verification.

3. Documents Typically Required
- SEE and +2 mark sheets and certificates (or transcripts)
- Character certificate from the last attended institution
- Migration certificate (if changing university/board)
- Citizenship certificate or birth certificate
- Passport size photographs
- Entrance exam admit card/result (if applicable)

4. Scholarships and Financial Aid
Many colleges offer merit-based scholarships (partial or full fee waiver) based on entrance exam rank or +2 GPA. Need-based scholarships and installment-based fee payment options are also common. Students should check directly with the college's admission office for the latest scholarship policy since it changes yearly.

5. Choosing the Right Program
Students should consider: accreditation of the university (TU, PU, KU, etc.), affiliated college reputation, lab/infrastructure facilities, faculty qualification, industry exposure (internships, projects), and placement record before choosing a college.

6. Important Tips
- Apply to multiple colleges to increase chances of admission.
- Prepare for entrance exams at least 2-3 months in advance.
- Keep all original and photocopied documents organized in a file.
- Confirm the refund/transfer policy before paying admission fees.
