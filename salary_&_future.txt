IT SALARY TRENDS AND FUTURE OUTLOOK (General Reference - Actual figures vary by country/company/experience, verify with live web search for latest numbers)

1. Nepal IT Job Market (Illustrative Ranges, entry to mid-level, local companies)
- Junior Software Developer: NPR 25,000 - 50,000/month
- Mid-level Developer (2-4 yrs exp): NPR 50,000 - 90,000/month
- Senior Developer / Team Lead: NPR 90,000 - 1,50,000+/month
- Data/AI roles tend to command a premium over standard web development roles due to skill scarcity.
- Remote work for foreign companies or freelancing can pay significantly higher (often 2-5x local rates) but requires strong English communication and portfolio.

2. Global/International Trends (General direction - always verify current numbers)
- AI/ML Engineers and GenAI/LLM specialists are among the highest-paid and most in-demand roles globally as of the mid-2020s.
- Cloud and DevOps engineers remain consistently high-demand due to continued cloud migration.
- Cybersecurity remains a critical shortage area worldwide, driving strong compensation.
- Full-stack developers remain broadly employable across company sizes.

3. Future Outlook for IT Careers
- Generative AI and LLM-based applications (chatbots, RAG systems, AI agents) are reshaping software development - familiarity with tools like LangChain, LangGraph, vector databases, and prompt engineering is increasingly valuable.
- Low-code/no-code tools are automating simpler development tasks, pushing developers toward more complex system design, AI integration, and architecture roles.
- Soft skills (communication, problem-solving, teamwork) combined with technical skills are increasingly valued as AI automates routine coding tasks.
- Continuous learning is essential - the half-life of specific technical skills is shrinking, so foundational computer science concepts plus adaptability matter more than any single tool.
- Remote work and global hiring continue to open opportunities for developers in Nepal and other countries to work for international companies without relocating.

4. How to Stay Competitive
- Build a portfolio of real projects (GitHub, personal website) rather than relying only on academic certificates.
- Contribute to open source or build end-to-end applications (like AI-powered tools, RAG systems, or automation agents) to demonstrate practical skill.
- Get relevant certifications (AWS/Azure/GCP, CompTIA, Google Data Analytics, etc.) to validate skills to employers.
- Network actively - LinkedIn presence, tech meetups, hackathons, and internships significantly improve job prospects.
